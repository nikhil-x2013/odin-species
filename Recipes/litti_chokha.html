Litti Chokha

Description:

The most famous food in Bihar, liti choka is a dish close to many a heart. This is a must have in Bihar just like Dal Bafla(in M.P) and Dal Bati in Rajasthan. Some people also call it baati chokha or chokha bati. You could actually call all of them as cousins. All of the baati are made with wheat flour over coal. Nowadays, you can bake them at home on an oven grill. Only, the curry served differs. While we serve the dal bati and dal bafla with lentils, this Bihar street food comes with vegetable mash. Another prominent feature is mustard oil. The mode of cooking changes from ghee in Madhya Pradesh and Rajasthan to mustard oil in Bihar food. Thus, changing from dairy to vegan.

Ingredients:

  • Whole meal flour
  • Pinch of baking soda
  • Ajwain / caraway seeds
  • Sattu
  • Pickle masala from any spicy pickle
  • chopped ginger and chillies
  • tomatos and onions
  • potato/sweet potato boiled or cooked in oven

Steps to make


Litti
  1. Mix the dry ingredients with oil and water. Knead a semi-tight dough for litti.
  2. Roll the dough and place the stuffing in the center.
  3. Close and shape it into a ball.
  4. Place the litti in a hot oven in a baking tray and Cook until they crack open and turn light brown.
  5. Brush them with ghee or oil if vegan, before serving.
Chokha
  1. Grill the aubergines/eggplant/brinjal and other vegetables in an oven at 180 C
  2. Potato grill for 30 mins and Peeled onions and tomato for 20 mins
  3. Peel all the vegetables and Mash it for the chokha. Mix with salt, oil and lemon juice.
  4. Heat mustard oil in a pan. Add ginger, garlic, chilly & cumin seeds.
  5. Serve it hot along with some sliced onions, lemons and chilly.
